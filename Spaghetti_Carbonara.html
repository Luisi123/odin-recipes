Spaghetti Carbonara recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 4 oz pancetta or guanciale, diced (or substitute with bacon)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Process:

  1. Cook the Spaghetti:

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.

  2. Prepare the Carbonara Sauce:

    • In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan, Pecorino, and a pinch of black pepper until smooth and creamy. Set aside.

  3. Cook the Pancetta:

    • In a large skillet, cook the pancetta (or bacon) over medium heat until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic during the last minute of cooking and cook until fragrant.

  4. Combine:

    • Add the drained spaghetti to the skillet with pancetta. Toss to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.
    • Remove the skillet from the heat and quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing the pasta to evenly coat. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time if you want a creamier sauce.
